intro: |
  Nishant Singh is an independent research consultant based in Delhi. He completed his PhD from the Department of Civil Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi in 2024, where his thesis --- [*New Bearings on Justice in Urban Mobility*](/work/phd-thesis/) --- developed conceptual and analytical frameworks grounded in the Capability Approach and critical theory to understand and address injustices in everyday urban mobility.

  His research interests span the politics of transport planning, road safety, urban inequality, and mixed methodologies. He works across academic research, policy engagement, and public advocacy --- contributing to projects with the WHO, Greenpeace India, and community organisations.

  Previously, he served as Project Scientist at the Transportation Research and Injury Prevention Centre (TRIP-C) at IIT Delhi, where he contributed to the *India Status Report on Road Safety: 2024*. He is the founder of [People's Resource Centre](https://www.prcindia.in), a knowledge initiative that supported research and advocacy around urban mobility, agriculture, rivers, and democratic governance in Indian cities from 2018 to 2021.

  He coordinates the [Public Transport Forum](https://publictransportforum.in/), India, and is a member of Sustainable Urban Mobility Network (SUM Net) India and Teachers Against Climate Crisis (TACC). His work on fare-free bus travel for women in Delhi and Mumbai has been featured in *The India Forum*, *Ideas for India*, *Down to Earth*, *Mongabay India*, and other publications.

  Nishant holds a B.Tech.-M.Tech. dual degree from IIT Kanpur, where his master's thesis examined [school trip mode choice behaviour](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0966692317303277/) in Kanpur city.
